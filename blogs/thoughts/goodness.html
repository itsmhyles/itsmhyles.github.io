On the Basis of Goodness

March 2025

Note: I will come back to fix these posts subsequently. For now, my goal is to write consistently- to get ideas going and be comfortable with putting myself out there.

It is not hard to imagine how this could have gone plenty of ways, frankly speaking, I urge you to read up on it as well, I know I would after this musing. But, as we have evolved over time, so has 'goodness', and if 'goodness' is a basis for reinforcement, then is it safe for us to blindly accept the notion of goodness that we have internalized?

If 'goodness' truly was necessary for our evolution, then it is not doubt that our notions of 'goodness' is tied to a societal level. If it is, is 'goodness' then a social construct that ensures order and ought to determine law? But for whose benefit? If society is to benefit from this universal set of constraints, then who benefits from society? This also detracts us from a fundamental question- does goodness have a purpose? Is 'goodness' meant to confer a benefit?

It seems a political question to pose, as much as it is a philosophical one. But take it as an exercise, and ask yourself- a couple of questions, with the first being, how much of your notion of 'goodness' is a conscious decision, and how much of it is a reinforced mechanism- a byfunction of societal construct.

Second, if and should a substantial portion of your answer be largely attributed to society, ask yourself if goodness ought to benefit society. If so, does society serve you? Is there a group, that society does not serve? Again, I disclaim that the purpose is not so much to question why we exist within society nor a questioning of why we partake in it. Such is a can of worms. I simply want to partake in a thought exercise of consciousness in thought.

If there is a group that society does not serve - is this simply another evolutionary byproduct? Or are there other rationales to such? What do you think?
